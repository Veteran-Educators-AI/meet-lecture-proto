Segment 1 — What a Mortgage Is (and why it matters)
A mortgage is a loan to buy a home. You pay it back with monthly payments over a long time, often fifteen or thirty years. The home itself is the collateral. If you don’t pay, the lender can take the home back through foreclosure.

Segment 2 — Core Vocabulary: Principal, Interest, Term
Every mortgage problem has three main knobs. Principal is the amount you borrow. Interest is the fee the bank charges for lending you money. Term is how long you have to pay the loan back.

Segment 3 — Fixed Rate vs ARM (Adjustable Rate Mortgage)
A fixed-rate mortgage keeps the same interest rate the whole time, so the monthly payment is predictable. An ARM can change after an introductory period, so the payment can go up. That can be risky if rates rise.

Segment 4 — Down Payment and Loan Amount
A down payment is money you pay up front. The loan amount is simple: home price minus down payment. A bigger down payment means you borrow less, which usually means a smaller monthly payment and less total interest.

Segment 5 — Interest Rates and Amortization
The interest rate is the cost of borrowing, and small changes can add up to thousands over a long loan. Each payment gets split into interest and principal. Early in the loan, most of your payment goes to interest. Later, more of the payment goes toward principal as the balance shrinks.

Segment 6 — What Determines the Monthly Payment
Monthly payment mainly depends on the loan amount, the interest rate, and the term. Borrow more and you pay more. A higher rate means you pay more. A longer term lowers the monthly payment, but you usually pay more total interest overall.
